# PLAN: Research Trump Executive Order Grants for Small Businesses

**Created:** 2026-05-03  
**Priority:** 🟡 Medium  
**Estimated Effort:** 8-12 hours  
**Domain:** business-research / government-grants  

---

## Objective

Research and document grants available to small businesses through Trump administration executive orders, including eligibility criteria, application processes, and deadlines.

---

## Phase 1: Executive Order Identification (2-3 hours)

### Tasks
1. **Identify relevant executive orders**
   - Search Federal Register for EO 13800-13900 range (Trump administration)
   - Focus on economic development, small business, deregulation
   - Key EOs to investigate:
     - EO 13809: Restoring Free Enterprise (2017)
     - EO 13853: Small Business Economic Growth (2018)
     - EO 13893: White House Initiative on Small Businesses (2019)
     - EO 13924: Regulatory Relief for Economic Recovery (2020)

2. **Create EO tracking spreadsheet**
   - EO number, title, date signed
   - Relevant sections for small business
   - Grant/funding mechanisms mentioned
   - Current status (active, superseded, revoked)

### Deliverables
- [ ] Spreadsheet with 10-15 relevant executive orders
- [ ] Summary document highlighting grant-related provisions

### Success Criteria
- All Trump-era EOs related to small business identified
- Clear mapping from EO to specific grant programs

---

## Phase 2: Government Database Research (3-4 hours)

### Tasks
1. **Search federal grant databases**
   - Grants.gov — search by agency (SBA, Commerce, EDA)
   - SAM.gov — contract opportunities linked to EOs
   - USAspending.gov — track funding allocations
   - SBA.gov — direct loan and grant programs

2. **Identify implementing agencies**
   - Small Business Administration (SBA)
   - Department of Commerce (EDA)
   - Department of Treasury
   - State-level economic development agencies

3. **Document funding mechanisms**
   - Direct grants vs. loan guarantees
   - Pass-through funding to states
   - Tax credit programs
   - Regulatory relief as indirect benefit

### Deliverables
- [ ] List of 20+ active grant programs linked to Trump EOs
- [ ] Agency contact information for each program
- [ ] Funding amounts and availability timelines

### Success Criteria
- Each identified EO mapped to at least one funding mechanism
- Contact information verified for top 10 programs

---

## Phase 3: Eligibility Criteria Documentation (2-3 hours)

### Tasks
1. **Extract eligibility requirements**
   - Business size standards (employee count, revenue)
   - Industry restrictions or preferences
   - Geographic limitations
   - Ownership requirements (minority, veteran, women-owned)

2. **Create eligibility matrix**
   - Rows: Grant programs
   - Columns: Eligibility criteria
   - Color coding: Green (meets), Yellow (partial), Red (excluded)

3. **Identify disqualifying factors**
   - Prior bankruptcies
   - Criminal history
   - Debarment status
   - Industry exclusions (gambling, lobbying, etc.)

### Deliverables
- [ ] Eligibility matrix (spreadsheet)
- [ ] Self-assessment checklist for business owners
- [ ] List of programs where eligibility is likely

### Success Criteria
- Clear yes/no determination possible for each program
- No ambiguity on basic eligibility requirements

---

## Phase 4: Application Process Mapping (2-3 hours)

### Tasks
1. **Document application workflows**
   - Required forms (SF-424, SF-424A, SF-424B)
   - Supporting documentation (business plan, financials, tax returns)
   - Submission portals (Grants.gov, agency-specific)
   - Review timelines and decision processes

2. **Create application timeline**
   - Pre-application steps (registration, DUNS number, SAM)
   - Application preparation (2-4 weeks typical)
   - Submission deadlines
   - Review period (60-120 days typical)
   - Award notification and acceptance

3. **Identify common pitfalls**
   - Incomplete applications
   - Missing signatures
   - Budget inconsistencies
   - Non-compliant formatting

### Deliverables
- [ ] Step-by-step application guide for top 5 programs
- [ ] Application timeline template
- [ ] Checklist of common mistakes to avoid

### Success Criteria
- Application process documented well enough to execute without assistance
- Timeline realistic with buffer for delays

---

## Phase 5: Synthesis and Recommendations (1-2 hours)

### Tasks
1. **Prioritize grant opportunities**
   - Rank by: funding amount, likelihood of success, effort required
   - Create "quick wins" vs. "stretch goals" categories

2. **Develop action plan**
   - Immediate steps (registrations, document gathering)
   - Short-term goals (submit 2-3 applications in 30 days)
   - Long-term strategy (build relationships, reapply if rejected)

3. **Create resource list**
   - SBA district offices
   - SCORE mentors
   - Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs)
   - Grant writers (if outsourcing)

### Deliverables
- [ ] Prioritized grant opportunity list (top 10)
- [ ] 90-day action plan with milestones
- [ ] Resource contact list

### Success Criteria
- Clear next steps identified
- First application ready to submit within 2 weeks

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| EOs revoked by Biden admin | High | High | Focus on programs still active regardless of EO origin |
| Grants already awarded | Medium | Medium | Identify rolling application programs vs. one-time |
| Eligibility too restrictive | Medium | Low | Cast wide net, apply to multiple programs |
| Application complexity | High | Medium | Engage SBDC or grant writer for top opportunities |

- Focus on programs that remain active regardless of political origin
- State-level implementations may have different names but same funding source
- Consider COVID-19 relief programs (PPP, EIDL) as separate track
